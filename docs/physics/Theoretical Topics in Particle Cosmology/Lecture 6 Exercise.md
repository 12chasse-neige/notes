# Lecture 6 Exercise

(1) Please show that the 4-point amplitude of 4 all-plus gluons vanishes at the tree level in the Yang-Mills theory.



(2) Please study the $u$-channel factorization of the 4-gluon amplitude $M_4(1^a_+, 2^b_+, 3^c_-, 4^d_-)$ and provide detailed steps leading to (13).

\[
\lim_{u\to 0} M_4 = [12]^2 \langle 34 \rangle^2 \frac{\gamma}{us}, \quad \gamma \equiv \sum_e \kappa^{ace} \kappa^{dbe} \tag{13}
\]


(3) Consider the scattering amplitude $M_4(1_{+j}, 2_{+j}, 3_{-j}, 4_{-j})$ of two massless spin-$j$ particles via exchanging a graviton through the minimal coupling. Please show that it is impossible to get consistent scattering for $j > 2$. [Hint: Dimensional analysis suffices.]



(4) Please find all 3-point amplitudes for a massless spin-$j$ particle emitting a graviton with minimal gravitational coupling.



(5) Please study the gravitational Compton scattering in (26). Using the result of the previous exercise and consistent factorization, please show that the gravitational coupling of this spin-$j$ particle must be equal to the graviton cubic self-coupling.
