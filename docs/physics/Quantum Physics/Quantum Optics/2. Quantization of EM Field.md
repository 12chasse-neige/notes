## Quantization of EM Field in a Cavity

\[
\frac{1}{c^{2}} \pdv[2]{}{t} E_{x} (z, t) - \pdv[2]{}{z} E_{x} (z, t) = 0 \\
E_{x} (0, t) = E_{x} (L ,t) = 0
\]

Here we use the integral of vector potential to solve this problem
\[
E_{x} (z, t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \dd{k} (A_{k} e^{- i \omega t + kz} + c.c.)
\]
Adding the boundary conditions on $A_{k}$, we could know that
\[
A_{k} = - A_{-k}
\]
and the allowed wave number is
\[
k_{n} = \frac{n \pi}{L}
\]
Using the discrete values, we can write the EM field in the form of 
\[
E_{x} (z, t) = 2i \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \left(A_{k_{n}} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} - c.c. \right)  \sin (k_{n} z)
\]

### Quantization

Transfering the amplitude into operators
\[
A_{k_{n}} \to \hat{A}_{k_{n}} \qquad A^{*}_{k_{n}} \to \hat{A}^{\dagger}_{k_{n}}
\]
We can define annihilation and creation operators
\[
\hat{a}_{n} = i \sqrt{\frac{2 L \epsilon_{0}}{\hbar \omega_{n}}} \hat{A}_{k_{n}} \quad \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} = - i \sqrt{\frac{2 L \epsilon_{0}}{\hbar \omega_{n}}} \hat{A}_{k_{n}}
\]
So we can use the annihilation and creation operators to express the electromagnetic field
\[
E_{x} (z, t) = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2 \hbar \omega_{n}}{L \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t }\right) \sin (k_{n} z)
\]

\[
B_{y} (z, t) = -i \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2 \hbar \omega_{n} \mu_{0}}{L}} \left(\hat{a}_{n}e^{- i \omega_{n} t}  - \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right) \cos(k_{n} z)
\]

The total energy of the EM field is
\[
\hat{H} = \int \frac{1}{2} \left(\epsilon_{0} E^{2} + \frac{B^{2}}{\mu_{0}} \right) \, \dd{\tau} \\ =
\frac{\hbar}{2} \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \omega_{n} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t }\right)^{2} + \frac{\hbar}{2} \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \omega_{n} \left(\hat{a}_{n}e^{- i \omega_{n} t}  - \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right)^{2} \\ =
\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} \{\hat{a}_{n}, \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}\} = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} (2 \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} \hat{a}_{n} + [\hat{a}_{n}, \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}])
\]
Considering the commutation rule for the annihilation and creation operators
\[
\comm{\hat{a}_{n}}{\hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}} = 1
\]
So the total energy is
\[
\hat{H} = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} (2 \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} \hat{a}_{n} + 1)
\]
Each mode can be viewed as a quantum harmonic oscillator and the total  energy is the sum of contributions from all of them.
