## EM Field in a Cavity

$$
\frac{1}{c^{2}} \pdv[2]{}{t} E_{x} (z, t) - \pdv[2]{}{z} E_{x} (z, t) = 0 \\
E_{x} (0, t) = E_{x} (L ,t) = 0
$$

Here we use the integral of vector potential to solve this problem
$$
E_{x} (z, t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \dd{k} (A_{k} e^{- i \omega t + kz} + c.c.)
$$
Adding the boundary conditions on $A_{k}$, we could know that
$$
A_{k} = - A_{-k}
$$
and the allowed wave number is
$$
k_{n} = \frac{n \pi}{L}
$$
Using the discrete values, we can write the EM field in the form of 
$$
E_{x} (z, t) = 2i \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \left(A_{k_{n}} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} - c.c. \right)  \sin (k_{n} z)
$$

### Quantization

Transfering the amplitude into operators
$$
A_{k_{n}} \to \hat{A}_{k_{n}} \qquad A^{*}_{k_{n}} \to \hat{A}^{\dagger}_{k_{n}}
$$
We can define annihilation and creation operators
$$
\hat{a}_{n} = i \sqrt{\frac{2 L \epsilon_{0}}{\hbar \omega_{n}}} \hat{A}_{k_{n}} \quad \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} = - i \sqrt{\frac{2 L \epsilon_{0}}{\hbar \omega_{n}}} \hat{A}_{k_{n}}
$$
So we can use the annihilation and creation operators to express the electromagnetic field
$$
E_{x} (z, t) = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2 \hbar \omega_{n}}{L \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right) \sin (k_{n} z)
$$

$$
B_{y} (z, t) = -i \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2 \hbar \omega_{n} \mu_{0}}{L}} \left(\hat{a}_{n}e^{- i \omega_{n} t}  - \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right) \cos(k_{n} z)
$$

The total energy of the EM field is
$$
\hat{H} = \int \frac{1}{2} \left(\epsilon_{0} E^{2} + \frac{B^{2}}{\mu_{0}} \right) \, \dd{\tau} \\ =
\frac{\hbar}{2} \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \omega_{n} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega_{n} t} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right)^{2} + \frac{\hbar}{2} \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \omega_{n} \left(\hat{a}_{n}e^{- i \omega_{n} t}  - \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t}\right)^{2} \\ =
\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} \{\hat{a}_{n}, \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}\} = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} (2 \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} \hat{a}_{n} + [\hat{a}_{n}, \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}])
$$
Considering the commutation rule for the annihilation and creation operators
$$
\comm{\hat{a}_{n}}{\hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}} = 1
$$
So the total energy is
$$
\hat{H} = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2} (2 \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} \hat{a}_{n} + 1)
$$
Each mode can be viewed as a quantum harmonic oscillator and the total  energy is the sum of contributions from all of them.

#### Energy Eigenstate

We can express the total energy eigenstates in direct product of  multiple-photon states, say
$$
\ket{N} = \ket{N_{\omega_{1}}} \otimes \ket{N_{\omega_{2}}} \otimes \ket{N_{\omega_{3}}} \otimes \cdots
$$
while the hamiltonian exerts on the eigenstate gives
$$
\hat{H} \ket{N} = \sum_{n} \hbar \omega_{n} (N_{n} + \frac{1}{2})
$$

#### Quadratures

Using the linear combination of the annihilation and creation operators, we can define amplitude and phase quadratures
$$
\hat{X}_{n} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}\right) \quad (\text{amplitude quadrature}) \\
\hat{Y}_{n} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} i} \left(\hat{a}_{n} - \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger}\right) \quad (\text{phase quadrature})
$$
which analogous to position and momentum of a classical oscillator.

## EM Field in the Free Space

Consider a ring cavity’s boundary condition, at this time
$$
\omega_{n} = \frac{2 \pi  cn}{L} \\
k_{n} = \frac{2 \pi n}{L}
$$
Quantize the electric field
$$
\hat{E}_{x} (z, t) = \sum_{n = \pm1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega_{n}}{2 L \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega_{n} t + i k_{n} z} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega_{n} t -i k_{n} z}\right)
$$
And now take the continuous limit ($L \to \infty$)
$$
k_{n + 1} - k_{n} \to \frac{2 \pi}{L} \dd{k}
$$

$$
\sum_{n = \pm 1}^{\infty} \to \int_{- \infty}^{\infty} \frac{L}{2 \pi} \dd{k}
$$

We define that
$$
\hat{a}_{k} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \pi}{\dd{k}}} \hat{a}_{n}
$$
and the commutation rule for $\hat{a}_{k}$ is
$$
\comm{\hat{a}_{k}}{\hat{a}_{k}^{\dagger}} = \frac{2 \pi}{\dd{k}} \delta_{n n'} \approx 2 \pi \delta (k - k')
$$


So the quantized electricmagnetic field is
$$
\hat{E}_{x} (z, t) = \int_{- \infty}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{k}}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega t + i kz} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega t - i k z}\right)
$$

$$
\hat{B}_{y} (z, t) = \frac{1}{c} \int_{- \infty}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{k}}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{n} e^{- i \omega t + i kz} + \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} e^{i \omega t - i k z}\right)
$$

If we ignore zero-point energy, we can write the hamiltonian as
$$
\hat{H} = \int_{- \infty}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{k}}{2 \pi} \hbar c |k| \hat{a}_{n}^{\dagger} \hat{a}_{n}
$$

## Narrow Band Approximation

For a laser with central frequency $\omega_{0}$ and half band width $\Omega$, where $\Omega \ll \omega_{0}$, so the electric field can be written as
$$
E^{\rightarrow}_{x} (z, t) = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{k}}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{k} e^{- i\omega t + i kz} + c.c.\right) \\ =
\int_{-\omega_{0}}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{\Omega}}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar (\omega_{0} + \Omega)}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{\Omega} e^{- i \Omega (t - \frac{z}{c})} e^{-i \omega_{0} t + i k_{0} z} + c.c.\right) \\ \approx
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{\Omega}}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega_{0}}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a}_{\Omega} e^{- i \Omega (t - \frac{z}{c})} e^{-i \omega_{0} t + i k_{0} z} + c.c.\right)
$$
Define $\hat{a} (t)$ as
$$
\hat{a} (t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{\dd{\Omega}}{2 \pi} \hat{a}_{\Omega} e^{- i \Omega t}
$$
So the electric field under approximation is
$$
E^{\rightarrow}_{x} (z, t) \approx \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega_{0}}{2 \epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{a} (t - \frac{z}{c}) e^{-i \omega_{0} t + i k_{0} z} + \hat{a}^{\dagger} (t - \frac{z}{c}) e^{i \omega_{0} t - i k_{0} z}\right)
$$
Using and the amplitude and phase quadratures
$$
\hat{X} (t) \equiv \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (\hat{a} (t) + \hat{a}^{\dagger} (t)) \\
\hat{Y} (t) \equiv \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} i} (\hat{a} (t) - \hat{a}^{\dagger} (t))
$$
The field can be rewritten as
$$
E_{x} (z, t) = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar \omega_{0}}{\epsilon_{0}}} \left(\hat{X} (t - \frac{z}{c}) \cos (\omega_{0} t - k_{0} z) + \hat{Y} (t - \frac{z}{c}) \sin(\omega_{0} t - k_{0} z)\right)
$$
