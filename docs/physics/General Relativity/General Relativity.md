## Review of Special Relativity

Lorentz Transformation
$$
\pdv{x'^{\mu}}{x^{\nu}} = \Lambda^{\mu} {}_{\nu}
$$

$$
\pdv{x^{\nu}}{x'^{\mu}} = \Lambda_{\mu} {}^{\nu}
$$

$$
\Lambda_{\nu} {}^{\kappa} \Lambda^{\nu} {}_{\sigma} = \delta^{\kappa} {}_{\sigma}
$$

Transformation rules
$$
V'^{\mu} (x') = \Lambda^{\mu} {}_{\nu} V^{\nu} (x) \\
V'_{\mu} (x') = \Lambda_{\mu} {}^{\nu} V_{\nu} (x)
$$
Define the Christoffel Symbol
\[
\Gamma^{\mu} {}_{\rho\sigma} = \frac{\partial x^{\mu}}{\partial \xi^{\alpha}} \frac{\partial^{2} \xi^{\alpha}}{\partial x^{\rho} \partial x^{\sigma}}
\]
and thus we can try to describe the motion for an accelerating particle
\[
\dv[2]{x^{\mu}}{\tau} + \Gamma^{\mu} {}_{\rho \sigma} \dv{x^{\rho}}{\tau} \dv{x^{\sigma}}{\tau} = 0
\]

## Equivalence Principle

At every Spacetime point in an arbitrary gravitational field, it is possible to choose a “local inertial coondinate system” such that, within a sufficient small region of the point in question, the laws of the nature takes the same form as in unaccelerated Cartesion coordinate system in the absense of gravitation.
